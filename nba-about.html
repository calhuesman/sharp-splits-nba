🏀 About Sharp Splits

How the game works, where the stats come from, and how the betting system is built.

What is Sharp Splits?

Sharp Splits is a stat-recall guessing game built around real NBA statistics. Each round, you're shown a real player from a real season between 2020-21 and 2025-26, and asked to guess their per-game stat average for that year. Which stat depends on position: point guards get quizzed on points per game or assists per game, wings get points per game, and forwards and centers get points per game or rebounds per game. You play five rounds per game, and your score is how far off your guesses were, added together. Lower is better.

On top of the core guessing game, Sharp Splits layers in a fun-money betting system, a cosmetic shop, achievements, a daily challenge that's identical for every player, and head-to-head matches you can play against friends or random opponents. None of it involves real money changing hands between players — the in-game bankroll is a scorekeeping and progression device, not a wagering product.

How a round works

  1. The reveal. Before you see the player, you're shown which season and which stat you're guessing — points, rebounds, or assists — since the position determines which stats are in play.
  2. The guess. You have eight seconds to type a decimal average, like 24.5 points per game. Being 2 points over or 2 points under counts the same — there's no partial credit for direction.
  3. The reveal. Your guess is compared to the real average, and the difference gets added to your running score for the game.
  4. Five rounds, one score. After five rounds, your total is locked in. That's your game.

The deck of players is drawn from six real NBA seasons, weighted so the most recent season shows up a bit more often than the others, without letting it dominate the mix. Within a single game, you'll never see the same player twice.

Because points per game naturally swings across a much wider range than rebounds or assists do, a points-based round is scaled down specifically for betting-threshold purposes, so a scoring-heavy hand doesn't unfairly wreck a bet compared to a hand full of rebounding or playmaking specialists. Your displayed score and the leaderboard always show your real, unscaled result — the scaling only affects whether certain bets resolve as wins or losses.

Where the data comes from

Every player and every stat average in Sharp Splits is a real, verified statistic pulled from Fox Sports' official NBA stats pages, covering the 2020-21 through 2025-26 regular seasons. This isn't simulated or randomly generated data — if the game tells you a player's season average, that's the actual number from that actual season. The pool currently includes several hundred individual player-season-stat combinations across scoring, rebounding, and assists, and it continues to grow as more seasons and more depth get added.

Because players change teams via trades and free agency, building this dataset accurately takes real verification work — checking that a player's listed team actually matches the team they played for during that specific season, not just whichever team they're on now, which is a surprisingly common source of error in quickly-assembled sports databases. Several corrections have been made along the way specifically to fix this kind of mismatch.

The betting system, explained

Every game gives you the option to place fun-money bets before you start, using bankroll you've built up through play — no real currency is ever involved. The bet categories are:

  • Total Score Under — bet that your combined five-round score will land under a given line. The line itself is personalized: it adjusts based on your own recent performance, refreshed periodically, so the game stays challenging as you improve. A hard ceiling also prevents the line from ever becoming too easy, even if your recent history looks unusually poor.
  • Best Round Under — bet that at least one of your five guesses will land within a tight margin of the real number.
  • Worst Round Under — the opposite bet: that even your sloppiest guess of the five stays under a threshold, rewarding consistency rather than one lucky guess.
  • Hot Hand — a bet that three or more of your five rounds will land within a small margin of accuracy.
  • Star Power — a pure-luck bet on whether at least one of the five players you're dealt is a recognized star player, independent of how well you guess.
  • Season Spread — another luck-based bet on how many different seasons show up across your five players.
  • Perfect Guess — a long-shot bet that pays out generously if you nail a player's exact stat average on any single round.

Bets use standard American odds notation (the same format used by real sportsbooks), so a bet listed at "+150" pays $150 profit on a $100 stake if it wins, and "-150" means you'd need to stake $150 to profit $100. All of this money is entirely in-game — it has no cash value and can't be withdrawn, exchanged, or converted to real currency in any way. The game also includes an adaptive difficulty system: if a player's bankroll grows unusually fast within a short window, payouts scale down for the rest of that window, keeping the game fair for everyone rather than allowing a lucky streak to spiral out of proportion.

Shop, achievements, and progression

Bankroll earned through play (and through the daily login bonus) can be spent in the in-game shop on purely cosmetic items — display flair, color themes, and similar visual customization — as well as gameplay tools like re-rolling a player you don't recognize, or paying for a scout report hint. Tools that affect your accuracy are clearly flagged and excluded from official "best score" tracking, so the leaderboard stays an honest measure of actual skill rather than how much bankroll someone spent on hints.

There's a full achievement system tracking milestones — win streaks, perfect guesses, daily challenge streaks, head-to-head duel records, and collection-style goals like owning every cosmetic flair. A prestige system lets committed players reset their bankroll in exchange for a permanent visual marker once they've hit a high bankroll milestone, similar to prestige systems in other progression-based games.

Daily Challenge

Once a day, every player who opens Sharp Splits gets served the exact same five players — the identity of that day's deck is generated the same way for everyone, worldwide, using the date itself as the seed. This means your Daily Challenge score is directly comparable to anyone else who plays that day, and there's a dedicated leaderboard just for that day's results. There's no betting on the Daily Challenge — it's built as a clean, bankroll-neutral mode focused purely on bragging rights and a day-streak you can build up over time.

Head-to-Head

You can challenge friends directly — inviting up to three others to a shared lobby where everyone plays the identical five players, with custom settings the host controls (which seasons to draw from, how long the per-round timer runs). There's also random matchmaking for a quick 1-on-1 against whoever else is waiting, using standard default settings. Like the Daily Challenge, head-to-head play doesn't touch your bankroll — it's a separate, bragging-rights-only mode with its own win/loss record tracking.

A note on the "betting" language

Sharp Splits uses sportsbook-style language — odds, lines, stakes — because that framing makes the accuracy challenge more engaging, not because any real money is involved. There is no way to deposit real currency, no way to withdraw in-game currency as cash, and no real-world monetary stakes of any kind tied to the outcome of a bet within the game. It's closer in spirit to a game of pretend-money poker among friends than to any regulated gambling product.
