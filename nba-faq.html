❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most about Sharp Splits.

Is this real-money gambling?

No. Sharp Splits uses sportsbook-style language — odds, lines, bets — because it makes the accuracy challenge feel more engaging, but every dollar in the game is entirely fictional. You can't deposit real money to get more in-game bankroll for free, and there's no way to withdraw or cash out your in-game bankroll. It functions more like a scorekeeping system with betting-flavored mechanics than an actual wagering product. (Some cash packs are available for purchase to support the game's development, but these simply add fun-money bankroll — they don't create any payout or winnings mechanism tied to real currency.)

How is my score calculated?

Each of the five rounds in a game compares your guess to the real per-game stat average for that player — points, rebounds, or assists depending on their position — and the absolute difference (how far off you were, regardless of direction) gets added to your running total. A perfect game — guessing every player's exact average — would score zero. Lower scores are better. There's no bonus for guessing high or low specifically; being 2 points over counts exactly the same as being 2 points under.

Why do point guards get different stats than centers?

The game matches the stat to the position it's most relevant for. Point guards get quizzed on points per game or assists per game, wings (shooting guards and small forwards) get points per game, and forwards and centers get points per game or rebounds per game. This keeps every round testing a stat that's actually meaningful for that player's role, rather than asking, say, a rebounding-focused center's assist average.

Where do the player statistics come from?

All player data is sourced from Fox Sports' official NBA statistics pages, covering the 2020-21 through 2025-26 regular seasons. We manually verify each player's team assignment for the specific season being used, since a lot of sports data sources default to showing a player's current team rather than the team they were actually on during a given historical season — a common source of inaccuracy we specifically check for and correct. Several such corrections have been made along the way as new seasons were added to the pool.

How often is new data added?

The player pool is actively and regularly expanded — both by adding depth to existing seasons (more players per stat category, per year) and, over time, by extending further back to cover additional NBA seasons. Positions, stat categories, and seasons that are currently thinner in the pool are a known, ongoing area of improvement.

Do I need an account to play?

Yes, a free account (email and password) is required so your bankroll, achievements, and leaderboard standing can be saved and synced across sessions. We don't sell or share your email address, and it's never shown publicly — only your chosen display name appears on leaderboards and to other players.

Can I change or delete my account?

Yes. You can rename your display name at any time from the home screen, and full account deletion is available as well. Deleting your account removes your saved bankroll, achievements, and history.

What's the Daily Challenge, and how is it different from a regular game?

Once per day, every player worldwide is served the exact same five players, generated the same way for everyone using that day's date — so scores are directly comparable across all players that day, with a dedicated leaderboard for it. Unlike a regular game, the Daily Challenge doesn't allow betting or bankroll-affecting shop tools; it's a clean, even playing field focused purely on accuracy, streaks, and bragging rights.

What are Head-to-Head duels?

You can challenge up to three friends to a shared match where everyone gets dealt the identical five players, with the host controlling settings like which seasons to draw from and how long each round's timer runs. There's also quick random matchmaking against another waiting player using standard default settings. Duels track a separate win/loss record and don't touch your bankroll.

Why does a points-heavy hand get treated differently in some bets?

Points per game naturally swings across a much wider range than rebounds or assists do — a player might average anywhere from 10 to over 30 points a game, while rebound and assist averages cluster in a narrower band. Treating a points-based miss identically to a rebounding or assist miss in certain bet categories would make those bets unfairly easy or hard depending on which stat you happened to draw. Where relevant, the game applies a scaling adjustment specifically for betting-threshold purposes — your displayed score and the leaderboard always show your real, unscaled result.

Is there a mobile app?

Sharp Splits runs entirely in your mobile or desktop browser — there's no separate app to download. It's built to work well on a phone screen without needing to be installed.

How do I report a bug or suggest a feature?

Sharp Splits is actively maintained and improved. If you notice incorrect player data, a bug, or have an idea for the game, reach out through the contact information on the site.
